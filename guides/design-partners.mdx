ImaRobot is actively looking for receiver-side design partners — developer platforms and API providers who want to shape how agent identity works before a standard gets written without them.

## What we're looking for

Platforms where AI agents are already calling your APIs — or will be soon. Especially:

- **Developer platforms** (Railway, Vercel, Netlify, Render)
- **Auth providers** (Clerk, Auth0, Okta)
- **Data platforms** (Supabase, PlanetScale, Snowflake)
- **Financial infrastructure** (Plaid, Stripe, Coupa)
- **Infrastructure / security** (Cloudflare, Datadog, PagerDuty)

## What you get

- **Free Enterprise tier** for 6 months
- Direct access to the founder — not a sales process
- Your platform's constraints become spec requirements, not afterthoughts
- Reference in NIST CAISI submissions as a contributing platform

## What we ask

- 3 calls over 60 days (45 min each)
- Share real agent traffic patterns or pain points
- Optional: co-author a case study after launch

## Why now

The NIST AI Agent Standards Initiative launched February 2026. The vocabulary for agent identity — what "scoped access" means, how revocation works, what audit logs look like — is being defined this quarter. Early design partners shape it. Everyone else adopts it.
